Phishing / Smishing

UPI / Banking Fraud

Scammers send fake payment requests or call impersonating bank officials asking for "KYC update." They collect UPI PIN to steal money directly.

HIGH RISK

10 Golden Cyber Safety Rules

Simple steps that can save your money, data, and peace of mind.

01
Never Share OTP, PIN, or Password
No bank, government, or company will ever ask for your OTP or PIN. Agar koi maange ‚Äî toh scam hai. Immediately disconnect the call.
BANKING
02
Use Strong, Unique Passwords
Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across sites. Use a password manager like Bitwarden (free) to keep track.
ACCOUNTS
03
Enable 2-Step Verification (2FA)
Enable two-factor authentication on Gmail, WhatsApp, banking apps, and social media. Even if your password is stolen, your account stays safe.
ACCOUNTS
04
Verify Before You Click Any Link
Hover over links to see the real URL. Check for misspellings like "sbi-bank-login.xyz" vs "sbi.co.in". When in doubt ‚Äî don't click.
PHISHING
05
Keep Devices and Apps Updated
Software updates patch security holes that hackers exploit. Enable auto-updates for your phone, apps, and antivirus software.
DEVICES
06
Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Financial Tasks
Never do banking, shopping, or enter passwords on public Wi-Fi (cafes, railway stations). Use mobile data or a VPN for sensitive work.
NETWORK
07
Check App Permissions
A flashlight app doesn't need your contacts or location. Review permissions before installing apps and remove unused ones regularly.
PRIVACY
08
Lock Your Devices
Always lock your phone with a PIN, pattern, or biometric. Enable remote wipe in case of theft using "Find My Device" (Android/iOS).
DEVICES
09
Be Skeptical of "Too Good to Be True" Offers
Free iPhone lottery, 50% return on investment, work-from-home earning ‚Çπ50,000/day ‚Äî agar kuch bahut acha lag raha hai, toh zaroor scam hai.
SCAMS
10
Report Cybercrime Immediately
If you are scammed, report within 24 hours at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930. Quick reporting increases chances of recovering your money.
